# coding

My career as a software developer started with my Master in Computational Linguistics and Multilingual Text Analysis at the University of Zurich.
In my thesis, I developed a Named Enity Recognition text processing pipeline (BOTANICAL NER) able to detect and extract scientific and vernacular plant names from German and English texts.
Using the developed tools and insights from this thesis, we applied this processing pipeline to digitized botanical works with the goal to make species-related information available as Linked Open Data (more INFO).
After my studies, I gained some experience in frontend web development and database maintainance for endangered species and geo-distributed data, such as the SPECIES WEB ATLAS.
Another research project involved developing a conversion pipeline for Linked Open Data for visualizing forest data (LD-LFI).

If you need a software developer for advanced data processing, code reviews or whatever helper script to automate a tedious task, feel free to reach out.

Skills:
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP) using Python
  • Frontend (JavaScript frameworks: Vue.js, Angular)
  • Databases (Oracle SQL)
  • Other: git, unix, RDF, OpenLayers, LaTeX

# teaching

Life is all about learning, it is up to us to see the world through new eyes that are willing to learn.

Besides some teaching experience in German "Deutsch als Fremdsprache" (DaF) at the Goethe institute in Bologna and in a 1:1 student refugees program, I am currently working on a new teaching project, "La Fattoria Didattica". Using the farm as a peaceful collective setting, we are developing an inclusive space for people from allover the world regardless of their diverse backgrounds. Problems are solved collectively, lessons are learned together - the day ends with a project completed and a smile on the face.

Please reach out, if you have a specific course topic in mind.

# excursions

Falling in love with our surrounding biodiversity and the marvels of nature is easy.
Teaching other people to do the same, is hard...


I noticed during some random hiking trips with friends and family how much I love telling people about trees and shrubs, about wild-growing herbs and their application for natural remedies, about identifying flowers and some easy tricks to distinguish one species from another.

Get in touch if you want to know more about the plants that surround you or if you just want to plan a tailored, informative hike with your work colleagues or family.

Trust me, it's going to be fun!

Qualifications:
